CatchLog

Privacy Policy

Last updated: 8 July 2026

This Privacy Policy explains what personal data CatchLog ("the app", "we", "us") collects, why, how it is stored, and the rights you have over it. CatchLog is a fishing-log Progressive Web App available at https://mr-glister.github.io/catchlog/.

In short: CatchLog stores your fishing data on your own device by default. If you sign in with Google, your data is also synced to your private cloud storage so it is available across your devices. We do not sell your data, and we do not use it for advertising.

1. Who is responsible for your data

The data controller for CatchLog is the individual operator of the app REINHARD FELLNER TRAINING & CONSULTING, based in Norway. You can contact us at reinhard.fellner@hotmail.com for any privacy-related question or to exercise your rights.

2. What data we collect

CatchLog only collects data you actively create by using it, plus environmental data automatically fetched to enrich your log entries.

3. Legal basis for processing (GDPR)

  • Performance of a service you request (Art. 6(1)(b)): storing and syncing the fishing data you create is what the app is for.
  • Consent (Art. 6(1)(a)): accessing your device location, camera, and signing in with Google all require your explicit permission, which you can withdraw at any time.
  • Legitimate interest (Art. 6(1)(f)): basic technical operation, delivering app updates, and keeping the service secure.

4. Where your data is stored

On your device

By default, all your data lives locally in your browser (localStorage and IndexedDB). If you never sign in, nothing leaves your device except the anonymous requests to public weather/tide APIs described below.

In the cloud (only if you sign in)

If you sign in with Google, your records and photos are synced to Google Firebase (Cloud Firestore and Firebase Storage), operated by Google. This lets you access your data across devices. Your data is stored in your own space and is not shared with other users. Firebase may process data on servers inside or outside the EU/EEA; Google provides GDPR-compliant safeguards (including Standard Contractual Clauses) for such transfers.

6. What we do not do

  • We do not sell or rent your personal data.
  • We do not use your data for advertising or profiling.
  • We do not share your individual fishing records, spots, or photos with other users or third parties, except the cloud storage provider needed to sync them for you.

7. Aggregated and anonymized data

We may create aggregated, anonymized statistics (for example, general patterns of catches by conditions across all users) that cannot identify you or reveal your specific spots. If we ever intend to use or share such aggregated data beyond improving the app, we will describe it clearly and, where required, ask for your consent first. 8. How long we keep data

Your data is kept for as long as you use the app. Local data remains until you delete it or clear your browser storage. Cloud data remains until you delete records or delete your account. When you delete your account, your synced records and photos are removed from cloud storage.

9. Your rights under GDPR

You have the right to access, correct, delete, restrict, and port your personal data, and to object to certain processing. In practice:

  • Access & portability: use the in-app JSON and CSV export to obtain a full copy of your data at any time.
  • Deletion: delete individual records in the app, or delete all local data via your browser; to delete synced cloud data, use the in-app delete/account-removal option or contact us.
  • Withdraw consent: revoke location or camera permission in your device settings, or sign out to stop cloud sync.

You also have the right to lodge a complaint with the Norwegian Data Protection Authority (Datatilsynet, datatilsynet.no).

10. Children

CatchLog is not directed at children and does not knowingly collect data from children under the age of digital consent (15 in Norway) without appropriate consent.

11. Security

Cloud data is protected by Google's authentication and access controls, so that only your signed-in account can access your records. No system is perfectly secure, but we take reasonable measures to protect your data.

12. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy as the app evolves. The "last updated" date above reflects the current version. Material changes will be communicated within the app where reasonably possible.
